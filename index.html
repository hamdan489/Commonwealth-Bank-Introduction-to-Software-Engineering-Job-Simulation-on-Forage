Tips to Improve Financial Cybersecurity

  1. Exercise Caution with Links and Emails

    • Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown sources, especially in unsolicited emails.

    • Instead, directly access your financial institution's website or app to verify any communications.

  2. Use Strong and Unique Passwords

    • Create long passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters.

    • Avoid using common phrases or easily guessable information.

    • Use different passwords for each account to minimize risk if one password is compromised.

  3. Browse Carefully

    • Ensure you are on a secure website when accessing your financial accounts online. Look for a web address that starts with "https" and has a key or closed padlock icon in the browser's status bar.

    • Avoid multitasking on multiple webpages while logged into your accounts. If you need to visit another site, use a different browser to reduce the risk of "session stealing."

    • Always click the "log out" button to properly terminate access to your financial account. Simply closing the browser or typing a new web address may allow others to re-enter and access your account.

  4. Enable Multifactor Authentication (MFA)

    Opt for multifactor authentication where available, requiring an additional verification step (e.g., SMS code) along with your password.

  5. Maintain Device Security

    • Install and regularly update security software, including antivirus and antispyware programs.

    • Keep your operating system and software up to date with automatic updates.

    • Consider using encryption and secure connections when accessing financial accounts.

  6. Use Your Own Device.

    • Avoid using public computers or unfamiliar devices to access financial accounts due to potential security risks.

    • Public computers may have malicious software that can capture passwords and PINs, compromising your account security.

    • If you must use another computer, remember to clear your browsing history, cache, and temporary internet files after logging out.

    • Use strong passwords, passphrases, or biometric authentication to protect your mobile devices and any financial apps you use.

  7. Use Secure Networks

    Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions. Use a trusted, encrypted network or a VPN (Virtual Private Network) for added security.

  8. Verify Websites and Companies

    Research companies and websites before sharing personal information or making transactions. Look for HTTPS in the web address to ensure encryption is used for data transmission.

  9. Be Mindful of Personal Information

    • Treat personal information like cash. Share it only when necessary and with trusted entities.

    • Be cautious of phishing attempts and fraudulent requests for personal information.

  10. Regularly Review Account Activity

    • Monitor your financial accounts regularly for unauthorized transactions or changes.

    • Review monthly statements promptly and report any discrepancies to your financial institution.

  11. Back Up Important Data

    Regularly back up important financial data on secure, offline storage to protect against data loss.
